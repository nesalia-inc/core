# Debate Points: Where the Agents Disagreed

*The Growth Hacker, Skeptic, DX Specialist, and Enterprise Architect had conflicting views. Here's how we resolved them.*

---

## Point 1: The Maintenance Angle

### The Disagreement

| Agent | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Growth Hacker | "Maintenance is a key differentiator. Emphasize it heavily." |
| Skeptic | "neverthrow had commits in Feb 2026. fp-ts is still maintained. The maintenance angle is overblown." |

### The Facts

- **neverthrow:** Last commit Feb 2026, but mostly bug fixes
- **fp-ts:** Still active but merging with Effect-TS (uncertain future)
- **@deessejs/fp:** Active, 48hr response time on issues

### Resolution

The maintenance angle is **valid but should be reframed**. Instead of:

> "We're maintained and they're not"

Say:

> "We respond faster and we build the features you request."

The Feb 2026 neverthrow commit was likely a bug fix, not a feature response. Emphasize responsiveness, not just maintenance status.

## Point 2: Video Content

### The Disagreement

| Agent | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Growth Hacker | "A single YouTube video could drive 50K downloads." |
| DX Specialist | "Before investing in video, make sure the first 5 minutes of onboarding is perfect. What's the point of driving traffic to a confusing homepage?" |
| Skeptic | "YouTube content has terrible ROI before product-market fit." |

### The Facts

- Creating a quality YouTube video takes 20-40 hours
- If the homepage confuses visitors, they'll bounce
- Video only compounds existing traffic (doesn't create it)

### Resolution

**DX Specialist wins this round.**

1. **Month 1-2:** Fix onboarding first (playground, migration guides, try/catch page)
2. **Month 3:** Create YouTube demo once the homepage is working
3. **Video content:** "30-second demo showing the aha moment"

Video is a multiplier, not a foundation.

## Point 3: The 500K Target

### The Disagreement

| Agent | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Master Plan | 500K weekly downloads in 12 months |
| Skeptic | "This is delusional." |

### The Facts

- neverthrow: ~1.5M weekly downloads after 7 years
- fp-ts: ~4M weekly downloads after ~10 years
- ts-results: ~168K weekly downloads after 5 years (then abandoned)
- @deessejs/fp: Currently negligible downloads

### Resolution

**500K is a stretch goal, not a target.**

| Timeline | Realistic Target | Stretch Goal |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Month 3 | 10K | 20K |
| Month 6 | 30K | 60K |
| Month 12 | 100K | 200K-500K |

500K weekly downloads would require viral adoption (HN front page, major company adoption, viral conference talk).

## Point 4: Influencer Outreach

### The Disagreement

| Agent | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Growth Hacker | "Cold outreach to Jack Herrington, Theo Browne could drive 50K downloads." |
| Skeptic | "Won't move the needle. These influencers get 100s of asks daily." |
| DX Specialist | "Before paying for influencer content, fix the product." |

### The Facts

- Cold outreach to influencers has < 1% response rate
- Influencers respond to: genuine interesting stories, not library promotions
- Organic mentions > sponsored content in TS community

### Resolution

**Don't cold pitch influencers.**

Instead:
1. Get 10-20 developers to blog about their migration
2. These blog posts get shared organically
3. An influencer notices the pattern
4. Influencer creates content organically

**The pipeline:** Real testimonials > influencer interest > content

---

## Point 5: Enterprise vs Developer Focus

### The Disagreement

| Agent | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Enterprise Architect | "Enterprise buyers are the only ones who pay. Focus on CTO trust signals." |
| Growth Hacker | "Individual developers drive viral adoption. Focus on devs." |
| Skeptic | "Enterprise takes 12-18 months to close. You'll run out of money." |

### The Facts

- Enterprise deals require: legal review, security review, POC, procurement (3-12 months)
- Individual developers drive npm downloads (the metric that matters for visibility)
- But individual devs don't pay for open source

### Resolution

**Both matter, but on different timelines.**

| Focus | Timeline | Metric |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Developer adoption | 0-6 months | npm downloads |
| Enterprise adoption | 6-18 months | contracts, testimonials |

**Strategy:** Drive developer adoption first (build credibility), then convert to enterprise customers.

## Summary: Resolved Positions

| Point | Resolution |
|-------|------------|
| Maintenance angle | Reframe as "responsiveness" not just "maintenance" |
| Video content | Month 3, after DX fixes |
| 500K target | 100K realistic, 500K stretch |
| Influencer outreach | Build organic pipeline, don't cold pitch |
| Enterprise vs devs | Developers first (downloads), enterprise later (contracts) |
